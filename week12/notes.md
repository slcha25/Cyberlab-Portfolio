## What I Learned

* In S35 Lab, I learned how to audit a GitHub repository systematically by verifying folder structure, artifact completeness, and documentation standards across all 12 weeks. I also learned the difference between the Cyber Kill Chain (7 steps, created by Lockheed Martin in 2011) and the Unified Kill Chain (18 phases, created by Paul Pols in 2017). The Unified Kill Chain is more comprehensive because it combines the Cyber Kill Chain with the MITRE ATT&CK framework and covers what attackers do after they get inside the system, not just how they get in.

* In S36 Lab, I learned how to produce a professional post-mortem document in APA style that covers all four phases of a red-to-blue operation: Reconnaissance, Exploitation, Forensics, and Hardening. I also learned the difference between Red Team (offensive — simulates attacker), Blue Team (defensive — monitors and responds), and Purple Team (collaborative — Red and Blue working together in real time to improve defenses faster).

* In this week, I also learned the 5 lab learning objectives that define a full red-to-blue security engagement: (1) active reconnaissance without prior target knowledge using nmap, (2) triage and remediate multiple vulnerabilities under time pressure, (3) execute a brute-force credential attack then switch to defender and extract forensic evidence from raw logs, (4) exploit a command injection vulnerability, establish a reverse shell, then harden the perimeter, and (5) produce a professional post-mortem report in APA format demonstrating analytical thinking across all four phases.

* In addition, I reviewed the entire Phase 1 curriculum and connected all concepts together — from Linux fundamentals and networking, through Python scripting, virtualization, identity management, reconnaissance, exploitation, web application security, digital forensics, and active defense — into a complete picture of what a security practitioner needs to know to operate across both offensive and defensive roles.

## Challenges & How I Solved Them

* The most challenging part of this week was writing the post-mortem document in APA style while covering all four phases of the red-to-blue operation at the same time. I had to organize my technical findings clearly and present them in professional academic format, which required me to think analytically about what happened at each phase rather than just describing the steps I followed.

* Understanding the difference between the Cyber Kill Chain and the Unified Kill Chain was initially confusing because both describe attacker behavior. I solved this by mapping the 18 Unified Kill Chain phases into 3 clear cycles (IN, THROUGH, OUT) which made it easier to see how much more comprehensive the Unified Kill Chain is compared to the original 7 steps.
